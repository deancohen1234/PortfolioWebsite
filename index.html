WHY THIS

The first thing I learned in college was how much I’d need to teach myself. Technology in the gaming and technology industry changes so quickly, projects begun in the spring can be outdated by fall. On the way to getting my degree in Gaming, Interactive Media and Mobile Technology, I learned a whole lot more than C++ and Unreal. I learned how to think and adapt, work independently and with teams, press on until I find an answer and, most of all, to never give up. There is no such thing as game over.

ABOUT ME

I was almost a chemist. Luckily for me, an internship with the U.S. Geological Survey sorting dirt and the new major of Gaming, Interactive Media and Mobile Technology at Boise State convinced me that my talents lay elsewhere. My first real job in the technology field was designing an augmented and virtual reality app for the Idaho National Laboratory’s data on electric cars. Working on projects like a game for Eating Disorders and VR Simulation of WW1 really solidified my passions for game development. I hope that one day the work I helped develop can aid in powering someone through a tough time in their life, just by giving them a small smile of fun and enjoyment.
