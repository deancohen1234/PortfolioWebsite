Dev Blog 12

Death and Poofs, September 2, 2019

Hello! So this week I worked on a couple different things. As the title explains, I worked on what happens when the player dies. I made a simple particle system that plays and set it up so if I add some extra animation to the player I can add that in without too much hastle. I also began adding a couple more frames to the run animation for the character, and I am planning on reworking their run so it looks less jank. For this next week I am hoping to actually do a bit of code refactoring for the scripts I've wrote so far, as well as try and do more art and animation. The art is definitely my weak suit so I am hoping to improve it and keep motivation as the weeks continue. Anyways that's all for this week. Thanks and have great day!

Dev Blog 11

Character Animation and Work, August 26, 2019

Salutations! So last week was a bit of hectic week for me. With 3 interviews and a lot of personal work to do I didn't focus as much as I could have on the game. But alas I got a small amount of work done! I began animating the new character for the game. The character's legs are a bit lanky and long, but overall it's not terrible. I plan on adding more frame to smooth out the motion as well as a little cloak of leaves for some style. For this week I also am planning on putting this character in the game and maybe also making a simple death animation and sequence. Overall though that is about it. Thank you for reading and I will see you all next week!

First Animation and New Concepts, August 19, 2019

Hello! I hope you're doing well! So for this week I did a combination of a couple of different things. I finally got a book on animation and made my first walk cycle and I'm pretty happy with it. Dev Blog 9

Level Testing and Animation, August 12, 2019

Salutations! So this week I worked on blocking out and testing how the movement system feels in an environment. Overall I think with some tweaking there is some real potential! I'm first testing it in a more closed environment with a tighter camera but I may this week try and make a level that's a little more opena and non-linear. I also added my first bit of new animation in a while. I'm using new software to make it and spent a fair amount of time coming up with a workflow but I think I have a solid system now. For this week I recently got a book on animation so my goal is to make a decent walking animation for the character. That's it for this week , thank you and have a great day!

Dev Blog 7

Time and Shakiness, July 29, 2019

Hello! This week I pretty much did all my work in one very productive night which worked out well! I added a camera shake system and began testing my main mechanic of wind gusting. To the wind gust I added a time slow effect which works to give the player more time to plan out their movement. I also created a basic background and some simple particle effects for the game. On top of that I met with my game design friend Aaron who looked at the game and gave some great advice on how to take it further. So for the coming week I am going to try and refine the gust system so it works consistently, and once that is good I will try and begin blocking out levels to see what is fun. This weekend I am planning on doing a game jam so I may not have as much done as I would like but I will see what I can do. Thank you all very much and have a great week!

Rejuvenate

Rejuvenate is a 2D platformer where a tree spirit must traverse through a dormant tree to bring back life to it. The game has a heavy focus on flow and serenity and is currently in development.
